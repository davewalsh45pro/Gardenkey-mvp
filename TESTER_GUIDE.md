# GardenKey — Adding a Plant (Tester Guide)

Thanks for testing GardenKey! Here's how to add a plant that isn't in the library yet.

## What you can do
You can create a **draft** for a new plant. You can't edit the shared plant library
directly — that's kept to one place so the information stays reliable for everyone
who scans a tag.

## Steps
1. Open **Create tester plant** from the GardenKey home page.
2. Fill in what you know:
   - What the plant is (name)
   - Roughly what type of plant it is
   - Where it's growing (pot, border, seed tray, etc.)
   - Light, watering, soil — just your own notes, nothing needs to be perfect
3. Tap **Email draft to Dave**. This sends your notes as a draft — it doesn't add
   anything to the shared library by itself.
4. That's it. Dave reviews drafts and adds approved ones to the library, usually with
   a bit more detail added (care specifics, seasonal jobs, common problems).

## A couple of things to know
- Your draft is just notes — don't worry about getting every field right.
- If you'd rather just tell Dave directly (message, call, in person) instead of using
  the form, that's completely fine too — the form exists to make it easier to capture
  details on the spot, not to replace talking to him.
- If two testers scan the same plant type, that's useful information too — it tells
  Dave which plants are actually likely to matter for the real product.
